Biology has been my passion since always. In 2016, I completed my BSc in Biology at the National and Kapodistrian University of Athens. Afte that I had my MSc in Bioinformatics at the University of Crete (2018). Bioinformatics play a great role in every level of modern biology; in ecosystem functioning as well. I am a member of lab42open at IMBBC-HCMR since 2017. In the framework of PREGO project, I am now conducting my PhD entitled: “Merging NGS data, knowledge aggregation and data integration techniques, along with ecological network analysis (ENA): an attempt to decipher microbial community ecology and ecosystem functioning by taking advantage of the hypothesis-generating method”. My research interests focus on microbial ecology and ecosystem functioning at the microbial dimension via systems biology approaches. I have developed PEMA, a pipeline for the analysis of metabarcoding data, which has been selected from LifeWatch - ERIC as the one for the analysis of the ARMS derived data.
